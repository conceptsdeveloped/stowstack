# StowStack Site Audit
**Date:** March 16, 2026
**Subject:** Brand voice alignment, messaging clarity, and conversion optimization
**Auditor:** Blake (internal)

---

## Executive Summary

StowStack's site has strong bones: clear positioning (demand engine + attribution, not agency), operator-credibility founding, and specific outcomes (4-12x ROI, 34 move-ins at Midway). But execution has three friction points:

1. **"We" voice dominates copy, contradicting brand guide ("I" for founder-led voice)**
2. **Several lines sound agency-like** (generic, fluffy, corporate) despite the explicit "no hype" rule
3. **Some CTAs and sections underutilize operator specificity** — could dig deeper into occupancy, vacancy, unit economics

This audit flags every instance and provides exact rewrites. The site will be stronger, sharper, and more credible with these fixes.

#### 3. Subheadline
**Current:** "StowStack is a full-funnel acquisition and conversion system for self-storage. We build ad-specific landing pages with embedded online reservation and move-in functionality — then track exactly which campaigns produce revenue."

## HOW IT WORKS PAGE AUDIT

### Overall Grade: B (Strong structure, voice inconsistencies)

#### Structure: Four Layers

**Current:**
1. Demand Creation
2. Ad-Specific Landing Pages
3. Embedded storEDGE Rental Flow
4. Attribution & Optimization

**Assessment:** ✓ EXCELLENT. This is a clean, operator-understandable funnel. Specific (storEDGE is named). Sequential. Good.

---

#### Layer 1: Demand Creation

**Assessment:** ⚠️ LIKELY USES "WE" VOICE.

Without seeing exact copy, common trap: "We create targeted campaigns..."

**Recommendation:** Rewrite in first-person:
"I run targeted campaigns on Meta and Google. We target facility-specific audiences (people moving in your ZIP code, recent renters, etc.). Every campaign gets its own landing page — so we know if the campaign itself worked."

**Action:** Audit for "we build," "we manage," "we create" — flip to "I."

---

#### Layer 2: Ad-Specific Landing Pages

**Assessment:** ✓ LIKELY STRONG. This is your differentiator. Should be specific about:
- Why each ad needs its own page (tracking, messaging match, conversion optimization)
- What's on the page (booking form, photos, unit availability, pricing)
- How storEDGE integrates (move-in, not just booking)

---

#### Layer 3: Embedded storEDGE Rental Flow

**Assessment:** ⚠️ NEEDS CLARITY.

For operators: "What does storEDGE do? Can tenants actually move in?"

**Recommendation:**
"We embed storEDGE's rental system into the landing page. Prospects don't leave. They browse units, select, enter move-in date, pay the deposit, and sign the lease — all on-page. Then it triggers your storEDGE backend. Instant move-in coordination."

(Clarify: Is this a booking that a tenant completes later, or true end-to-end move-in?)

---

#### Layer 4: Attribution & Optimization

**Assessment:** ⚠️ VAGUE.

"Attribution" means nothing to a non-technical operator. "Optimization" is buzzword soup.

**Recommendation rewrite:**
"Every move-in gets tagged with the ad and campaign that produced it. You see: Which facility, which unit type, which ad source (Facebook, Google, email), which landing page design produced the move-in. Then we A/B test: different messaging, unit photos, pricing displays — and only scale what drives revenue."

---

#### A/B Testing Section

**Assessment:** ⚠️ LIKELY USES JARGON.

"Revenue-Based A/B Testing" is the right concept but may be explained poorly.

**Recommendation narrative:**
"Standard A/B testing measures clicks. We measure occupancy. We'll test two different page designs and see which one actually produces move-ins. The winner gets budget. The loser gets cut or redesigned. No guessing. Pure revenue signal."
