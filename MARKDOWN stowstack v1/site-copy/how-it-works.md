# How It Works

---

## Section Intro

### Four layers. One closed loop. Ad impression to signed lease.

StowStack is not a single service. It is a system. Four layers that feed each other — creating demand, catching it on the right page, converting it through an embedded rental flow, and attributing every move-in back to the exact ad that produced it.

Here is what each layer does and why it matters.

---

## Layer 1: Demand Creation

### Most operators wait for renters to find them. We go get them.

There are three kinds of demand in self-storage:

**Demand you create.** Meta ads put your facility in front of people before they ever search for storage. Someone scrolling Facebook sees your ad, realizes they need storage for their boat this winter, and clicks. That customer did not exist in the market five minutes ago. You created them.

**Demand you capture.** Google PPC catches people already searching — "climate controlled storage near me," "10x10 unit Paw Paw MI." These customers have intent. They are comparing options right now. You show up first.

**Demand you recapture.** Retargeting brings back people who visited your page but did not reserve. They saw your ad. They clicked. They left. Now your ad follows them until they come back and finish.

Most agencies pick one channel. SEO shops wait for organic traffic to build over 6-12 months. Google-only platforms capture existing searches but never create new demand. StowStack runs all three channels because the operator who fills units fastest is the one generating demand, capturing it, and recapturing it simultaneously.

Meta ads are our primary channel. It is the lane nobody else in self-storage owns.

---

## Layer 2: Ad-Specific Landing Pages

### Your Google ad and your Facebook ad should not go to the same page. Ever.

This is where most operators lose money. Every ad — regardless of channel, audience, or offer — sends traffic to the same destination. Your homepage. Or worse, a default system rental page with no branding, no offer context, and no reason for the customer to trust you.

StowStack builds a dedicated landing page for every campaign. Each page has:

- **Its own headline** matched to the ad that brought the customer there
- **Its own offer** aligned with the specific audience and intent
- **Its own tracking** so every visit, click, and conversion ties back to the campaign
- **Its own embedded rental flow** so the customer can reserve or move in without leaving
- **Its own conversion data** so you know exactly how that page performs

**Real examples of how this works:**

A Google ad targeting "climate controlled storage in Paw Paw" sends the click to `stowstacksite.com/climate-pawpaw-a`. That page talks about climate control. It shows climate-controlled unit photos. It highlights temperature protection. It offers a climate-specific promotion. The customer sees exactly what they searched for.

A Facebook ad promoting "first month free on 10x10 units" sends the click to `stowstacksite.com/10x10-offer-b`. That page leads with the offer. It shows 10x10 unit pricing. It answers the question "what fits in a 10x10?" It makes claiming the offer the only logical next step.

A retargeting ad for someone who browsed but did not reserve sends the click to `stowstacksite.com/finish-your-rental-c`. That page acknowledges they have been here before. It removes friction. It gives them one clear path to finish what they started.

**Three different customers. Three different intents. Three different pages.** That is how conversion rate compounds over time.

## Layer 3: Embedded storEDGE Rental Flow

### Your customer reserves a unit without ever leaving your page.

Here is what happens at most facilities: a customer clicks your ad, lands on your page, decides they want to rent, clicks "Reserve Now" — and gets bounced to a generic storEDGE rental page. Different branding. Different layout. Different URL. The trust they built with your ad and your page disappears.

StowStack embeds the storEDGE rental and move-in flow directly inside each landing page. The customer stays on your branded page the entire time. They see your facility name. Your colors. Your offer. And they complete their reservation right there.

**The split is clean:**

StowStack owns the front end — branding, copy, layout, trust elements, speed, conversion optimization, pixel tracking, and campaign segmentation.

storEDGE owns the transaction — real-time unit availability, reservation processing, move-in completion, and payment processing.

Your customer gets a fast, branded, trust-building experience. You get a completed reservation. storEDGE handles the backend. Everybody wins.

## Layer 4: Attribution and Optimization

### You will know exactly which ad produced which move-in.

This is the part no other company in self-storage can deliver. Because every ad maps to its own landing page with its own tracking, StowStack reports the complete path from ad impression to signed lease.

**What you see in your reporting:**

- Which ad generated the visit
- Which landing page the customer saw
- Which offer they responded to
- Whether they reserved
- Whether they completed move-in
- Cost per reservation
- Cost per move-in
- Conversion rate by campaign, ad, audience, keyword, and creative
- Return on ad spend by creative

No more guessing. No more "the campaign is performing well" with nothing to back it up. You see the numbers. You see which ads fill units. You see which pages convert. We kill what does not work and scale what does.

## A/B Testing

### Every test is measured against move-ins. Not clicks.

Because StowStack controls the page and tracks actual reservation behavior, we can run real A/B tests and pick winners based on revenue outcomes.

**What we test:**

- Headline A versus headline B
- Offer A versus offer B
- Page layout A versus page layout B
- Urgency messaging versus informational messaging
- Climate-focused copy versus price-focused copy
- Long-form pages versus short-form pages
- Trust badges versus no trust badges
- Facility photography versus lifestyle imagery

Most agencies A/B test ad creative and measure which one gets more clicks. We test the entire funnel — the ad, the page, the offer — and measure which combination produces more signed leases.

The winner is not the one people click on more. The winner is the one that fills more units.
