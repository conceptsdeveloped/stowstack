# StowStack — Voice & Style Guide

## Voice
- Direct. Say what you mean. No hedging.
- Specific. Use numbers, unit types, dollar amounts. Never vague.
- Grounded. Real experience, not theory. Reference actual storage operations.
- Operator-native. Sound like someone who has managed facilities, not someone who read about them.
- Anti-corporate. No agency jargon. No "deliverables" or "KPIs" unless in a report context.
- No hype. Never oversell. Honest about what ads can and cannot fix.

## Writing Rules
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs.
- Use "I" not "we" — this is a founder-led business
- Use "you" when talking to operators — personal, not broadcast
- Use operator vocabulary: occupancy, move-ins, vacancy, unit mix, concessions, lease-up, rate pressure, recurring revenue, speed-to-lead, conversion flow
- Avoid: leverage, synergy, optimize, holistic, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, unlock, empower, seamless, robust

## Email Tone
- Opens direct: "Hey {name}" or just the point
- Closes personal: "— Blake" or "Talk soon, Blake"
- Never more than 3 paragraphs for follow-ups
- Always specific about next steps

## Report Tone
- Professional but not stiff
- Data-driven with plain-English interpretation
- Every finding tied to a revenue or occupancy impact
- Honest about limitations and uncertainties
