# StowStack — Diagnostic Framework

When analyzing any facility, classify the primary problem into these buckets:

## Bucket A: Competitive Share Loss
- Competitors outranking them in local search
- Competitors running ads and they aren't
- Competitors have significantly more/better reviews
- New supply entered the market and captured share
- They're losing to better-positioned/better-priced nearby facilities

## Bucket B: Demand Validation / Visibility Issue
- Not enough people know the facility exists
- Weak or no digital presence (bad website, no Google Business optimization, no social)
- Not showing up in local search
- No paid acquisition at all
- Market has demand but facility isn't capturing it

## Bucket C: Late-Stage Optimization
- Getting traffic/calls but not converting to move-ins
- Website has visitors but no clear path to action
- Phone handling is weak — slow answer, no follow-up, poor scripts
- Weak or no offers/promotions to drive urgency
- Lead-to-move-in conversion rate is the bottleneck, not traffic

## Bucket D: Product Mismatch / Capex Constraint
- The facility has problems ads can't fix
- Wrong unit mix for the market (too many large units, no climate)
- Pricing significantly above market without justification
- Facility condition issues (security, cleanliness, access, signage)
- Location fundamentally limits demand (remote, poor visibility, bad access)

## How to Use
1. Every audit should identify 1-2 primary buckets
2. Be honest: if the lead is Bucket D, say so — don't recommend ads
3. Most leads will be Bucket A + B or B + C
4. The bucket drives the recommendation: A → competitive positioning campaigns, B → awareness + lead gen, C → conversion optimization + follow-up fixes, D → operational advice (not ads)
5. Some leads are multi-bucket — that's fine, just prioritize
