When writing for StowStack:

1. Read context/voice-and-style.md first — every time
2. Use "I" not "we" — this is a founder-led business
3. Short sentences. Short paragraphs. No walls of text.
4. Lead with outcome (occupancy, move-ins, revenue) not mechanism (ads, targeting, impressions)
5. Use operator vocabulary: occupancy, move-ins, vacancy, unit mix, concessions, lease-up, rate pressure, recurring revenue, speed-to-lead, conversion flow
6. Never use: leverage, synergy, optimize (unless in a technical ad context), holistic, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, unlock, empower, seamless, robust, game-changer, next-level
7. Be specific with numbers — "15 empty 10x10s at $120/mo = $1,800 in lost monthly revenue" beats "significant vacancy losses"
8. For ad copy: max 40 char headlines, max 125 char primary text, clear CTA, specific to facility
9. For emails: 3 paragraphs max for follow-ups, always end with a clear next step
10. For reports: every finding paired with a "so what" tied to revenue or occupancy impact
11. For the website: outcome first, specialization second, mechanism third
12. Never sound like an agency. Sound like a smart operator who also happens to know ads.
