# StowStack: Top 10 Objection Responses

## 1. "I already have a website."

Having a website and having a *conversion machine* are different things. Your site was probably built to look nice and explain what you offer — but it wasn't built to convert ad traffic into move-ins in days. StowStack landing pages are engineered specifically for paid ads: they strip away navigation clutter, highlight occupancy status, and embed the rental flow directly. You don't replace your website. You run ads to a purpose-built landing page that captures the hot leads your regular site misses.

## 2. "SEO is enough."

SEO is slow and passive — it takes 6-12 months to see real traction, and it only works if people are already searching for self-storage in your area. Most prospects don't search until they need a unit *now*, and by then they've already talked to 3 of your competitors. Paid ads let you reach intent buyers before they search, put your property in front of them first, and convert them the same week. SEO is a long-term play; ads are how you fill units this month.

## 3. "I tried Facebook ads and they didn't work."

Most operators run ads wrong — they point traffic to their homepage, target a massive radius, and use generic creative. Then they spend $500-$1,000 and quit when nothing happens. StowStack fixes that: we use ad-specific landing pages (built for the ad, not the other way around), tighter targeting, real-time A/B testing, and full-funnel tracking so you see exactly which ads produce move-ins. It's not that ads don't work in self-storage. It's that execution matters. A lot.

## 4. "I don't trust agencies."

Fair. Most agencies are account-manager factories that blur your ad spend, obscure results, and lock you into long contracts. I'm not an agency — I'm an operator like you with 7+ years in this business. I run this for myself and my partners' facilities too, so I have skin in the game. You're month-to-month, you see full-funnel attribution, and I talk to you directly. No account managers, no fine print.

## 5. "It's too expensive."

The math is simple: a move-in is worth $1,200–$1,800 in lifetime value (12-month average stay at $100–$150/month). At $41 average cost per move-in, you pocket $1,150–$1,750 per unit. The service is $750–$1,500/month retainer plus ad spend. If you fill just one unit per month from ads, you're profitable. Most operators see 3–5 in the first month. The question isn't cost — it's whether you can afford *not* to fill vacant units.

## 6. "I'm already at 90%+ occupancy."

Congrats. But 90% occupancy means you're leaving 10+ units empty — that's 10+ leases you're not collecting rent on. At $100–$150/month per unit, 10 vacant units is $1,000–$1,500 in lost monthly revenue. Even at a high occupancy rate, a single campaign can find those last 5–10 units and add $5,000–$7,500/month to your bottom line. When you're that full, the ROI on finding stragglers is insane.

## 7. "I can just do this myself."

You can try. But running ads means building landing pages, setting up conversion tracking, analyzing which creatives work, optimizing budgets in real-time, and scoring leads to prioritize hot prospects. That's a full-time job. The math: your time is worth $150–$300/hour (opportunity cost as an operator). Spending 10–15 hours per week on ads costs you $1,500–$4,500/month. StowStack is $750–$1,500/month *plus* you're not the bottleneck. It's cheaper to pay us than to do it yourself.

## 8. "My property management software already handles marketing."

Most PMS platforms include email templates and a basic website — that's not marketing, that's infrastructure. None of them run paid ads, build conversion-optimized landing pages, or do real-time A/B testing. They're meant to *manage* units and tenants, not *fill* them. If your PMS's marketing features were actually filling vacancies, you wouldn't have empty units. We do the one thing that actually works: run ads that convert.

## 9. "I don't want to be locked into a contract."

Then don't be. StowStack is month-to-month — no long-term contracts, no early-exit fees, no fine print. If we don't deliver results, you can walk in 30 days. I want you staying because we're filling units, not because you're trapped. Most operators see results in days or weeks, not months, so there's no reason to lock anyone in.

## 10. "I need to think about it / talk to my partner."

That's reasonable. But while you're thinking, your vacant units are costing you money. A single open unit for 30 days costs you $3,000–$4,500 in lost rent — that's the whole cost of StowStack for the month, plus profit on top. If you have 2–3 vacant units, every day you wait costs you hundreds. Talk to your partner, do the math, and let me know. But let me know soon — your occupancy problem is only getting worse.
