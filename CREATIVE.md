# CREATIVE.md — StowStack Creative Directive

This document is the single source of truth for all content generated through StowStack. Every ad, headline, video prompt, landing page, email, and piece of copy flows through the principles defined here. If it conflicts with a template default, this file wins.

---

## Voice & Identity

StowStack produces advertising for self-storage facilities. We are not the brand — the facility is. Our job is to make their brand look like it has a world-class marketing team behind it, without StowStack's fingerprints showing.

**The voice we write in:**
- Confident but never aggressive. We don't shout. We don't use exclamation marks unless the copy genuinely earns one.
- Conversational but precise. We write like a smart friend who happens to know exactly what you need — not like a salesperson.
- Warm without being saccharine. Storage is emotional — people store their lives. We respect that without exploiting it.
- Local and specific. Generic copy is invisible. We name the neighborhood, reference the weather, acknowledge the real reasons people need storage in THIS market.

**What we never do:**
- All caps headlines (except single-word emphasis: "FREE" or "NOW")
- Cliche storage puns ("Don't have a storage meltdown!")
- Stock marketing language ("state-of-the-art facility," "unbeatable prices," "we're #1")
- Fear-based urgency that isn't true ("Only 2 units left!" when there are 40)
- Anything that would make the facility look desperate

**What we always do:**
- Lead with what the customer gets, not what the facility has
- Use the facility's actual data (rating, reviews, pricing, features) as proof points
- Write headlines that work without context — someone scrolling should understand the value in 3 seconds
- Match the platform's native tone (TikTok is casual, Google Search is intent-driven, Meta is thumb-stopping)

## Creative Standards

Every piece of content should pass this test: *Would a creative director at Mother, Wieden+Kennedy, or Droga5 be embarrassed by this?* If yes, rewrite it.

**Headlines:**
- Maximum 7 words when possible. Every word earns its place.
- The best headline is a complete thought, not a setup for body copy.
- If you can replace the facility name with any other and the headline still works, it's too generic.
- Good: "Your garage called. It needs a break."
- Good: "4.8 stars. 247 families trust us with their stuff."
- Bad: "Affordable Storage Solutions in Your Area"
- Bad: "We Offer Climate-Controlled Units"

**Body copy / primary text:**
- First sentence does 80% of the work. If they stop reading after one line, they should still get the point.
- Social proof is the most powerful tool we have — ratings, review counts, years in business, number of happy tenants.
- Specific numbers always beat vague claims ("$49/month" beats "affordable," "4.8★ from 312 reviews" beats "highly rated").
- Write for scanners, not readers. Short paragraphs. Fragments are fine.

**CTAs:**
- Clear, specific, low-friction. "Reserve Your Unit" not "Learn More" (when reservation is the goal).
- One CTA per ad. One clear next step.
- The CTA should answer "What happens when I click this?" — "Get Your Free Quote" tells them exactly what's next.

## Ad Theory & Conversion Principles

**The storage purchase journey is short.** Most people who search for storage need it within days, not months. Our ads should match that urgency without manufacturing it.

**Hierarchy of persuasion for self-storage:**
1. **Proximity** — "5 minutes from you" is the strongest hook. Storage is a convenience purchase.
2. **Social proof** — Ratings and reviews are trust signals. A 4.8★ rating in the headline outperforms any clever copy.
3. **Price clarity** — "Starting at $49/mo" removes the biggest objection. Vague pricing creates friction.
4. **Ease** — "Reserve online in 60 seconds" or "No long-term lease" removes commitment fear.
5. **Emotional relief** — "Finally, room to breathe" or "Your stuff, safe and sound" — the emotional payoff of solving the problem.

**Minimum viable click path:**
Every ad should be designed to convert in the fewest possible steps:
- **Ideal:** Ad → Landing page with embedded reservation → Done (2 clicks)
- **Acceptable:** Ad → Landing page → Facility page → Reserve (3 clicks)
- **Unacceptable:** Ad → Homepage → Navigate to units → Select unit → Fill form → Confirm (5+ clicks)

The ad copy should pre-qualify the click. If someone clicks, they should already know the price range, the location, and what they're getting. The landing page confirms and converts — it doesn't re-sell.

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram Feed)
- **Primary text:** 80-125 characters. Conversational. One insight, one proof point.
- **Headline:** Under 40 characters. Bold claim or specific offer.
- **Image/Video:** Thumb-stopping in the first 0.5 seconds. No text-heavy graphics — Meta's algorithm penalizes them.
- **Targeting note:** Reference the local area. Meta's Andromeda system rewards relevance signals.
- **Compliance:** No misleading claims, no before/after implications that suggest guaranteed results, no discriminatory housing language (storage is not housing but adjacent — be careful with "family" targeting).

### Instagram Stories / Reels
- Vertical (9:16). Full-bleed visual.
- First frame must hook — no slow builds.
- Text overlays: big, readable, centered. Max 6 words on screen at once.
- CTA at the bottom third (swipe-up zone).

### Google Search Ads
- **Headlines:** Match search intent exactly. If they searched "10x10 storage unit Kalamazoo," the headline should contain those words.
- **Descriptions:** Include price, rating, and differentiator. This is not the place for clever copy — it's the place for relevant, specific information.
- **Sitelinks:** Drive to specific unit pages, not the homepage.
- **Quality Score matters:** Ad relevance + landing page experience + expected CTR. Our copy must match the keyword intent precisely.

### TikTok (Organic)
- Authenticity > production value. Polished corporate content dies on TikTok.
- Hook in the first 1 second. "POV: your garage looks like this" with a visual.
- Trending audio when relevant, but don't force it.
- Storage-relevant content themes: decluttering, moving tips, organization ASMR, facility tours, before/after.
- Local hashtags + niche hashtags > broad ones.

### Google Business Profile
- Posts should be timely (seasonal, promotional) and include a photo.
- Update weekly. Consistency signals activity to the algorithm.
- Respond to every review — good and bad. The response is marketing.

## Video Content Direction

All video content — whether AI-generated, slideshow, or produced — should embody:

**Visual aesthetic:**
- Warm, muted color palette with subtle texture. Not sterile, not oversaturated.
- Intentional negative space. Not every frame needs to be full.
- Slow, deliberate movement. Confidence is quiet.
- Natural light preferred. If artificial, warm and soft — never harsh.

**Pacing:**
- Let moments breathe. A 5-second video should feel like it has exactly the right number of ideas in it — usually one.
- Text appears with purpose, not decoration. If words are on screen, they're doing work.
- Transitions should feel inevitable, not showy.

**What a StowStack video should feel like:**
Not a storage ad. A moment of calm in someone's chaotic feed. The viewer should feel a subtle sense of order, relief, possibility — and then see the facility name.

## Compliance & Platform Terms

### Meta Advertising Policies
- No discriminatory targeting based on housing, employment, or credit (self-storage is adjacent — avoid "family" as a targeting category if used in housing context).
- No misleading claims about availability or pricing that could change.
- Landing pages must match ad claims. If the ad says "$49/mo," the landing page must show $49/mo prominently.
- Andromeda/Lattice ranking rewards: high engagement rate, low negative feedback, relevant audience matching, fresh creative rotation.

### Google Ads Policies
- No keyword stuffing in ad copy.
- Display URL must match landing page domain.
- No misleading extensions or callouts.
- Responsive Search Ads: provide diverse headlines that don't repeat the same claim.

### General
- All pricing in ads must be current and verifiable.
- "First month free" or similar promos must be actually active at the facility.
- No fake urgency ("Only 3 left!") unless verified from real inventory data.
- Review counts and ratings must be current (within 30 days).
