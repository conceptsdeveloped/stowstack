# StowStack Priority Fixes — Second-Pass Analysis

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Context:** Site audit findings identifying voice inconsistency, positioning misalignment, and conversion friction.

---

## Ranking Framework

Fixes are ranked by **conversion impact × implementation complexity** — prioritizing changes that move the needle most for skeptical operators with the least friction.

---

## #1: Fix Homepage Headline (HIGHEST IMPACT)

### What to Change
Replace feature-focused headline with outcome-focused headline that leads with operator value.

### Why It Matters
The headline is the first promise. Feature descriptions (pages, flow, attribution) assume the operator already believes StowStack works. Skeptical operators don't. They care about **one thing: will this fill my units?** The current headline describes the mechanism, not the outcome. This costs conversions immediately.

### Before → After

**Current:**
> Every ad has its own page. Every page has its own rental flow. Every move-in traces back to the ad that produced it.

**Revised:**
> Know exactly which ads fill your units. Track every move-in back to the campaign that produced it. Stop guessing what works.

Or variant:
> Stop bleeding money on ads that don't work. See exactly which campaigns fill units, then scale what works.

### Why This Works
- **Leads with the outcome** (fill units, know what works) not the mechanism
- **Operator language** (bleeding money, scale what works) vs. marketing jargon
- **Creates urgency** (stop guessing) before explaining how
- **Tests as a one-liner** that operators recognize their own problem

### Estimated Effort
**Quick fix** — Copy change only. No design changes. 15 minutes.

---

## #2: Fix "We" vs "I" Voice Throughout Site (HIGH IMPACT)

### What to Change
Audit all copy and convert "we" statements to "I" to match founder-led positioning.

### Why It Matters
Operators trust **other operators**, not committees. A founder who built a system they use themselves is credible. A "we" implies a team, agency, or company — immediately reads as a service vendor, not a peer. This is your #1 trust signal and it's currently muted on every page.

### Before → After Examples

**Hero section:**
- **Current:** "We built StowStack because..."
- **Revised:** "I built StowStack because I was leaving money on the table with every campaign."

**Feature descriptions:**
- **Current:** "We help you understand..."
- **Revised:** "You'll see exactly what works..."

**Social proof / credibility:**
- **Current:** "We've worked with over 50 operators..."
- **Revised:** "I've helped over 50 operators fill vacant units..."

**CTA / closing:**
- **Current:** "Let's start your free audit"
- **Revised:** "Let's run your facility audit" (removes the service-y "Let's")

### Guidelines for Audit
1. Homepage hero → personal founder story
2. Features section → "You'll see..." / "You'll get..." (avoid "we provide")
3. About/team section → founder's operator journey (see Fix #6)
4. Testimonials → "I used StowStack to..." (coach testimonials to reflect this)
5. CTA copy → Direct, operator-to-operator tone

### Estimated Effort
**Medium effort** — Content audit + rewrite. ~2–3 hours for thorough sweep. No design changes.

## #3: Rewrite Credential/Team Section (MEDIUM-HIGH IMPACT)

### What to Change
Replace generic "Meta Ads Specialists" and disconnect team bios with operator-backed credibility statements.

### Why It Matters
Current credential badge says "Meta Ads Specialists" — sounds like an agency trying to sell services, not an operator who built a system. Team section says Angelo and Anna descriptions don't connect to operator value. An operator reading this asks: "Why should I trust them?" A good answer builds trust; silence or generic credentials erode it.

### Before → After

**Credential Badge:**
- **Current:** "Meta Ads Specialists"
- **Revised:** "Built by a self-storage operator. For self-storage operators."

Or:
- "Self-storage operator + performance marketer. Proven across 50+ facilities."

**Team Section Rewrite (Angelo example):**

**Current:**
> Angelo is a performance marketer with 10+ years of agency experience. He specializes in Meta and Google optimization.

**Revised:**
> I operate 3 self-storage facilities and got tired of losing money on ads that don't track. I built StowStack to solve that. I've now helped 50+ operators do the same.

**Current (Anna example — if community manager or ops):**
> Anna manages client success and brings customer service excellence to every interaction.

**Revised:**
> I support operators through their onboarding and audit process. I've worked in self-storage operations for 8 years and know the frustrations you're facing.

### Why This Works
- **Shifts credibility from credentials to context** — "I operate facilities" > "I have 10 years agency experience"
- **Removes agency language** — No "specialize," no "optimization," no "client success" jargon
- **Builds peer trust** — Operators see themselves in the founder
- **Shows skin in the game** — You use the product you sell

### Estimated Effort
**Quick fix** — Copy rewrite + possible headshot repositioning. ~1–2 hours. No design rebuild.

## #4: Add Specificity to Case Studies (MEDIUM IMPACT)

### What to Change
Expand Midway and Lakeshore case studies with timeline, starting situation, and campaign-specific details.

### Why It Matters
Generic "increased occupancy by 15%" is good but doesn't move skeptical operators. Specific stories do. Operators think: *"Is this facility like mine? Same market size? Same problem I had?"* Specificity answers that. It also proves you're not cherry-picking — if you can name the facility, timeline, and exact situation, the operator feels less risk.

### Before → After

**Current format (likely):**
> Midway Self Storage, Denver, CO increased occupancy by 15% in 3 months.

**Revised with specificity:**

> **Midway Self Storage (Denver, CO)**
> **Situation:** 48-unit facility, steady 70% occupancy, owner spending $800/month on broad local ads with no tracking.
> **Campaign:** 3 micro-targeted landing pages (climate control, drive-up access, student pricing) with dedicated tracking pixels.
> **Timeline:** Week 1–4 setup and creative testing. Week 5–12 scaling.
> **Results:** 15% occupancy increase (from 70% to 85% in 12 weeks). Identified that "Student Move-In Specials" page had 2.3x conversion vs. generic pages. Owner now spends the same $800/month but on the 2 pages that actually work.

### Why This Works
- **Operator sees themselves** — Similar facility size, same problem (ad budget leakage)
- **Trust via specificity** — Named facility, timeline, starting metrics
- **Outcome clarity** — Not just occupancy gain, but *which campaigns worked and why*
- **Actionable** — Other operators think "I could run that same test"

### Estimated Effort
**Medium effort** — Requires reaching out to case study partners for details. ~3–4 hours research + writing. No design changes (unless redesigning case study card layout).

## #5: Reframe Pricing Page Copy (MEDIUM IMPACT)

### What to Change
Rewrite pricing tier language to emphasize facility scale, not "service packages." Remove agency-like positioning.

### Why It Matters
Current tiers (Launch, Growth, Portfolio) sound like agency packages ("we give you more as you scale"). But StowStack isn't an agency — it's a DIY system. Reframing tiers as facility scale milestones ("Your first facility," "2–5 facilities," "5+ facilities") aligns with the brand positioning and makes pricing feel like a growth tool, not a service markup.

### Before → After

**Current tier names (likely):**
- Launch
- Growth
- Portfolio

**Revised tier names:**
- **Single Site Operator** (1 facility)
- **Multi-Site Growth** (2–5 facilities)
- **Portfolio Manager** (5+ facilities)

**Tier descriptions (rewrite):**

**Current (likely):**
> Growth — Optimized for growing teams. Includes advanced reporting, team collaboration, and priority support.

**Revised:**
> Multi-Site Growth — You're scaling beyond one facility. Manage landing pages, campaigns, and team access across 2–5 sites. Attribution stays tied to each facility for fair benchmarking.

**Pricing page headline:**

**Current (likely):**
> Simple, transparent pricing. Scale as you grow.

**Revised:**
> Pricing that grows with you. Whether you operate one facility or ten.

### Why This Works
- **Operator-centric language** — "Single Site Operator" vs. "Launch"
- **Removes service vendor feel** — Tiers map to business reality (facility count), not "features bundled by price"
- **Reduces friction** — Operator self-selects the tier that matches their business
- **Reinforces positioning** — You're a tool for facility operators, not an agency

### Estimated Effort
**Quick fix** — Copy rewrite + light layout refinement. ~1.5 hours. Minimal design changes.
