Conversion went from 6.2% to 8.7%.

One facility. 200 more contacts per quarter just from match between ad and landing page.

Your generic homepage isn't built for ad traffic. It's built for someone already thinking about storage. The ad is the first moment of intent. Match it.

## Post 3: The Move-In That Proved Attribution Matters

**Hook:** I spent $2k on Facebook ads and couldn't figure out if they worked.

This was 2020. I was tracking *everything*. Click-through rate. Cost per click. Page views. Bounce rate.

None of it told me whether anyone actually moved into a unit.

I had 300 clicks. Maybe 5 rentals. Maybe 20. I genuinely didn't know.

So I started asking new tenants: "How did you find us?"

Got this answer a lot: "Saw your Facebook ad. But I didn't rent until 2 weeks later when my lease ended."

Attribution gap. The ad wasn't the last click. It was the *first* click.

Now I track the entire funnel. Ad click → landing page visit → form submission → move-in date → actual occupancy.

That's full-funnel attribution. When you know this, you can say: "These ads cost me $820 per move-in and generate $1,200+ LTV tenants."

Most storage operators are still guessing. That's why they kill ad campaigns that actually work.

## Post 5: Why 8.7% Landing Page Conversion Isn't Magic

**Hook:** My landing pages convert at 8.7%. That sounds good. Here's why it's actually the minimum.

Storage is a high-intent category. People searching "self storage" are ready to act. They're not tire-kicking.

If your storage landing pages aren't converting at 6%+, something's broken. Not because 8.7% is impressive. But because 6% is table stakes.

What moves the needle:
- Match between ad and page (I mentioned this already)
- Embedded rental flow (no leaving the page to start an application)
- Social proof (real reviews, move-in videos, occupancy %)
- Urgency (current move-in specials, availability count)
- Clear next step (big button, one form field, no friction)

But here's the thing: Conversion rate doesn't matter if nobody's seeing the ads.

You could have 20% conversion. Send 10 people to it per month. That's only 2 leads.

I care about total move-ins, not conversion rate. I'd rather get 100 people to a 6% page (6 moves-in) than 10 people to a 15% page (1.5 moves-in).

The math is more important than the percentage.

## Post 6: The Revenue Per Lead Mindset

**Hook:** If you're still measuring "cost per lead," you're thinking like an agency.

I used to track cost per form submission. $50 per lead was "good."

Then I realized: Not all leads are the same.

Someone who needs climate control and stays 8 months is worth more than someone looking for a 30-day unit.

So I flipped the metric. Now I track **revenue per lead**.

A $50 form submission might generate $0 (no-show, never moved in). Or it might generate $12,000 (stayed 12 months, referrals, premium unit).

This is why I A/B test on *revenue*, not clicks.

Ad A: 100 clicks, 8 conversions, $1,200 revenue each = $9,600 total
Ad B: 80 clicks, 6 conversions, $800 revenue each = $4,800 total

Ad A wins. More revenue, even though it has a lower conversion rate.

This changed how I spend money. Stopped optimizing for CTR. Started optimizing for LTV.

Most facilities don't track this because it's harder. You need CRM data + occupancy data + revenue data connected.

But if you're not doing this, you're losing money on ad spend that *looks* good but generates low-quality tenants.

## Post 8: The Landing Page Iteration I Didn't Expect to Win

**Hook:** I tested a landing page with no video. Destroyed the one with a 3-minute facility tour.

Assumption: People want to see the facility before they move in.

Reality: They want to know move-in cost and availability. Now.

My "losing" page:
- 3-minute tour video
- Photo carousel
- Feature list (climate, 24/7 access, etc.)
- Form at the bottom

Conversion: 6.2%

My "boring" page:
- Headline ("Climate-controlled storage from $29/month")
- 2-3 benefit bullets
- Move-in special (if running one)
- Form above the fold
- Reviews

Conversion: 8.7%

People came from an ad. They decided in 0.5 seconds whether this was what they wanted. The video was slower than the decision.

This is why I iterate. Every assumption I have about what converts gets tested against what actually converts.

I've been wrong 7 times out of 10.

That's not failure. That's data.

## Post 9: The Competitor Nobody Talks About

**Hook:** Your biggest competitor isn't another storage operator. It's someone's buddy in their phone.

People don't search "self storage near me" in a vacuum.

They ask their network first. "Hey, anyone know a good storage place?"

Then they Google.

So when I send someone an ad, they're already thinking about storage. But they're also asking people they trust.

A $41-per-move-in tenant who stays 8 months and refers 3 friends is worth $3,600 in incremental revenue. A $41-per-move-in tenant who churns in 2 months is worth $300.

This is why full-funnel attribution matters. It helps me identify which ads bring long-term, quality tenants.

And it helps me build a network effect. Good tenants become your best marketers.

Most facilities are still playing the volume game. More ads, more leads, lower quality.

I'm playing the quality game. Fewer ads, better tenants, more referrals.
