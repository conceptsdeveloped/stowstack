## ADDITIONAL NOTES FOR IMPLEMENTATION

### Design Principles
- **Hero should be above the fold** — Clean, direct, no hero image needed. White space. Bold headline
- **Section layout** — Each section is full-width, light gray alternating background (white, light gray, white, etc.)
- **Typography** — Sans-serif. Headline 48px, subhead 20px, body 16px. Short paragraphs (2–3 lines max)
- **CTAs** — Primary CTA (green, brand color). Secondary CTAs (outline, darker text). Sticky CTA at bottom of viewport on mobile
- **Mobile** — Single column. CTAs full-width on mobile
- **Trust elements** — Case study section should include operator names, facility type, and metrics. No logos needed unless operator agrees

### Conversion Mechanics
- **Primary conversion goal:** "Get a Free Facility Audit" form (email, facility name, current occupancy)
- **Secondary goal:** "Book a Call" (Calendly embed or Vercel serverless function redirect)
- **Form submission:** Trigger Resend transactional email (audit + auto-reply)
- **Post-conversion:** Redirect to thank-you page with audit PDF link and call booking confirmation

### Messaging Tone
- Operator-to-operator (use "I" — Blake)
- Direct. No marketing fluff
- Specific numbers (8.7%, $41, 34 move-ins)
- Address pain points head-on (generic agencies, scattered tools, no attribution)
- Solution is not sexy — it's practical and measurable

### Call-to-Action Flow
1. **Hero CTA:** "Get a Free Facility Audit" (primary goal)
2. **Secondary CTA:** "Book a Call" (for operators ready to talk)
3. **Bottom CTA:** Repeat both CTAs with final copy push
4. **Sticky mobile CTA:** Persistent green button at bottom on mobile
