# StowStack Discovery Call Script
**20-minute intro call for storage operators** | Blake Burkett (founder, active operator)

---

## OPENER (2-3 minutes)
*Goal: Build rapport as a peer operator, not a salesman. Get them comfortable and sharing.*

**Opening (natural, warm tone — you're calling a peer)**

"Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on. I'm Blake Burkett — I run a couple facilities in Michigan and got sick of the marketing game, so I built something to fix it. But before I get into that, I'm curious about you. How long have you been in storage?"

*(Listen to their answer. Find a genuine point of connection — years in business, location, size of portfolio, whatever.)*

**Follow-up (leans into shared experience)**

"That's [solid/great/good run]. Yeah, so I've been in storage since [your timeline], and what drove me nuts was always the same thing — you throw money at marketing, units fill up, but you never really know *why*. Like, did that unit move because of that Facebook ad or because someone drove by? No idea. Anyway, that's kind of what StowStack solves, but I don't want to dump that on you yet.

Tell me about your operation — how many units, and what's your setup like right now?"

*(Let them talk. This is about them, not you. You're gathering context.)*

---

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (8-10 minutes)
*Goal: Expose their current marketing inefficiency and occupancy gaps. Listen more than you talk.*

### **Question 1: Current Marketing Reality**
**"So right now, how are you filling your units? What does that look like?"**

*What they might say:*
- "Word of mouth and Google" → *They're relying on organic, leaving money on the table*
- "Signs on the road" → *Zero tracking, no data*
- "Google Local Services" → *They know about paid, but only one channel*
- "We tried Facebook once" → *Past experience, likely negative*

**Dig deeper based on their answer:**
- If organic-only: "Are you getting calls from that? What percentage would you say, roughly?"
- If ads: "Cool. How's that working? You hitting your targets?"
- If multiple channels: "Out of those, which one actually moves the needle for you?"

**What to listen for:**
- How many different channels they're using (more = more confusion)
- Whether they sound confident about their results (they won't be)
- If they mention expense without ROI ("Yeah, we spend money but...") ← gold

---

### **Question 2: Attribution / The Blind Spot**
**"Here's a real question — when a unit rents, how do you actually know what drove it? Like, what's your system for tracking that?"**

*What they'll likely say:*
- "We just... ask the customer" ← unreliable, depends on their memory
- "We tag our ads differently" ← manual, error-prone
- "Honestly, we don't really know" ← most common, admission of the problem
- "We use [some analytics platform]" ← might not connect to actual move-ins

**Follow-up (curious, not condescending):**
- "So if someone fills out your online form and then calls, do you connect those dots?"
- "Do you track which ad they clicked from all the way through to move-in?"

**What to listen for:**
- Lack of a system (this is your hook)
- Frustration with past attempts to track
- The phrase "we should probably track that better" ← pain admission

---

### **Question 3: Occupancy Gap**
**"What's your occupancy looking like right now, and where do you want it to be?"**

*They'll give you two numbers — actual vs. target.*

**Do the math with them (conversational, not salesy):**
"So you're at [X]% and want [Y]%? That's [gap] units. At what, $1,200-1,500 per move-in that's worth $[math] a month to you, yeah?"

*(Don't make them do the math. You do it. It lands harder.)*

**Follow-up:**
- "Are you okay waiting on organic to fill those, or are you actively trying to close that gap?"

**What to listen for:**
- Whether they see it as a real problem or "just how the market is"
- Urgency level (essential for close)
- Whether they've tried paid strategies before

---

### **Question 4: Past Ad Experience**
**"Have you played with paid ads — like Google, Facebook, TikTok — before?"**

*Likely scenarios:*

**If yes:** "What happened? Did it work or did you kill it?"
- If it worked: "Cool, so you know the channel works. What made you stop?" ← maybe budget, maybe they didn't scale right
- If it didn't: "Yeah, what went wrong? Spend too much? No conversions? Bad traffic?" ← dig into the real problem

**If no:** "What's kept you from trying it?"
- Budget concerns? Skepticism? Don't know where to start?

**What to listen for:**
- Specific problems (not just "it didn't work") — e.g., "leads were garbage" or "paid too much per lead"
- Whether they blame the channel or their execution (important distinction)
- Hidden belief that ads "don't work for storage" ← you'll address this

---

### **Question 5: Current Online Flow**
**"Walk me through your online rental process. Someone goes to your website or sees your ad — what happens next?"**

*Listen to how they describe it:*
- Is there a landing page? Just the main site?
- Can people book online or do they have to call?
- Do they capture email/phone before the call?
- What's the conversion rate (they probably won't know)?

**Follow-ups based on their answer:**
- "So every lead has to call you?" ← friction, loss of leads
- "Are you capturing email before they call?" ← qualification, nurture opportunities
- "Do you know how many people hit that page but don't convert?" ← almost certainly not

**What to listen for:**
- Friction in the flow (required phone call, no online booking)
- Lack of lead capture (emails, phone numbers)
- Single landing page problem (same page for all ads/channels)

---

## TRANSITION TO PITCH (3-4 minutes)
*Goal: Connect their specific pain points to StowStack's solution. This is NOT a feature dump.*

**Bridge statement (based on what they said):**

Pick the biggest pain you heard and reflect it back:

*Example 1 (attribution):* "So it sounds like your biggest frustration is really that when a unit rents, you don't actually know which marketing dollar caused it. You're kind of flying blind on ROI. Is that fair?"

*Example 2 (volume):* "Sounds like you've got plenty of occupancy, but filling that last [X]% is just... slow. You know paid ads can work, but your current flow just isn't set up for it."

*Example 3 (experience):* "And you tried Google Ads once but the leads weren't qualified or it got expensive fast. So now you're back to hoping word of mouth keeps you full."

**Then connect to StowStack (conversational):**

"That's exactly why I built StowStack. We do three things:

1. **Ad-specific landing pages** — not one page for everything. Each ad gets its own page, designed for that specific angle (price, location, unit size). Massively higher conversion.

2. **Full conversion tracking** — from ad click all the way through move-in. So you actually know: 'That TikTok ad cost me $45 and generated a $1,400 move-in.' Not guessing.

3. **Built-in booking/reservation** — people can book or reserve a unit right on the landing page. No friction. The ones who call already want to move in.

The whole point is: you stop throwing marketing money at the wall and actually *measure* what works. Then you scale the winners."

**Quick story/example (if it fits):**

*(Optional: quick 1-2 sentence example of a small operator or facility of theirs similar to them getting results)* — "We've got operators filling units at like $40-50 per move-in through paid ads, but only because they can actually see the data."

**Then get their reaction:**

"Does that sound like something that would help with [the specific problem they mentioned]?"

*(Wait for their response. Don't keep talking.)*

---

## HANDLING "WHAT'S THE COST?" (if it comes up early)
*Goal: Don't evade, but redirect to the math so price doesn't feel like a barrier.*

**If they ask before you've done the pitch:**

"Great question — we can definitely talk about that. But I want to make sure it even makes sense first, yeah? Like, if a move-in is worth [their number, e.g., $1,500 to you], and we can help you get them at $40-50 each, the math is pretty obvious. But let me ask you this first: are you getting *enough* leads right now, or is it more that the leads you do get are expensive or hard to convert?"

*(This reframes cost as ROI, not expense. Gives them a reason to care about the next step.)*

**If they push on price anyway:**

"Look, our model is usage-based because every facility is different. Anywhere from $400-2K depending on volume and complexity. But honestly, if a single move-in pays for the whole setup, it's not really the cost that's the question — it's whether it works for you. And I'd rather show you a free audit first so you can see the actual math for *your* facility. Sound fair?"

---

## CLOSE TO NEXT STEPS (2-3 minutes)
*Goal: Get a specific commitment. Not vague "let's talk later." Actual next step.*

**The ask (based on where they are):**

**If they're interested and engaged:**

"Here's what makes sense — I run a free facility audit. Takes like 30 minutes. I look at your current occupancy, marketing spend, online flow, all of it. Then I show you: 'Here's what you're leaving on the table, and here's how StowStack changes that for your specific operation.' No pressure. You'll know whether it's a fit or not. Can we schedule that?"

**If they're interested but hesitant:**

"Fair. Look, worst case you spend 30 minutes and get some free insights about your marketing. Best case you see a path to fill units faster and cheaper. What does your schedule look like next week?"

**If they seem skeptical:**

"I get it. Before you decide anything, let's just look at the numbers for your facility. Audit is free, and you can see for yourself whether the ROI makes sense. Can't do better than that, right? When's good for you?"

**Lock in the time:**

Don't hang up without a calendar invite or specific day/time.

"Great. I'm sending you a calendar link — let's do [day at X time]. Bring any data you have on your current occupancy, marketing spend — even rough numbers are fine. We'll walk through it together."

**Alternative close (if they're not ready but not rejecting):**

"No worries. At minimum, let me send you a quick one-pager on how storage operators are using paid ads right now. Then if you want to grab 30 minutes, we'll do the audit. Sound good?"

*(This keeps the door open without high pressure.)*

---

## TONE REMINDERS

- **You're a peer, not a salesman.** You run facilities. You've been frustrated too.
- **Listen more than you talk.** The questions are the tool. Let them fill the silence.
- **Be specific.** "It averages $40-50 per move-in" beats "it's affordable."
- **Don't memorize.** This is a guide, not a script. Use your own words.
- **If they get defensive**, acknowledge it. "I know, tracking is annoying. That's why this is automated."
- **Stay curious.** "How does that work for you?" is better than "You should try [thing]."

---

## RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

- They're not concerned about occupancy ← wrong time, different problem
- They can't articulate their occupancy/revenue goal ← not ready
- They've tried ads multiple times and blame the channel, not execution ← might be a learning curve issue
- They seem happy with current organic flow ← respect that, don't force it

---

## IF THEY SAY "LET ME THINK ABOUT IT"

"Sure, that's fair. One thing though — while you're thinking, grab your occupancy numbers for the last 3 months and your rough monthly ad spend if you have it. When we do the audit, those numbers make everything make sense. I'll send you a quick form to fill out beforehand — takes 5 minutes. Deal?"

*(Get small commitments. The form pre-call is a filter and a lock-in.)*
